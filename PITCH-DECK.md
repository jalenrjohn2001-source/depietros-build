## The 10-second pitch

This is the version you say first. Whether it's a walk-in, a phone call, or a DM.

> "Hi, I'm JJ. I build websites for restaurants on the Shore. I already made one for DePietro's. Want to see it? Takes 10 seconds."

That's it. Hand them the link or open it on your phone.

**Why this works:**
You're not asking for time to build something. You already built it. The owner just has to react to a thing in front of them. People can do "react" in 10 seconds. They can't do "schedule a meeting" in 10 seconds.

---

## When they say "yeah let me see"

Open the site on your phone. Hand the phone over. Stay quiet. Let them scroll.

Watch their face. The first three sections do the work:
- Hero with the pizza and the headline
- Story section ("Thirteen seats…")
- Menu with the real Bronx Bomber + Maryland Crab + their actual prices

If they say "wow" or "this is nice" or "you made this?" — you're closing.

If they say nothing or "huh" — ask them: *"What do you think? Honest."*

---

## When they say "this is great" — the close

Pick the right tier based on their vibe.

**If they're excited and ready to move:**
> "I can install the real version Thursday. I block off 8 hours. I meet you at 11am before lunch rush, confirm your menu prices, take 10 photos with my phone, and you have a live website by 7pm. $1,500. Paid when it goes live."

**If they want it faster:**
> "I can do a next-day rush. You pay $2,400 and I have it live by tomorrow night. Same scope — full custom site, your menu, your logo, your address, mobile-ready, online ordering integration. Just 24 hours instead of one day's work."

**If they're nervous about price:**
> "Lighter option: I patch what you have. 4 hours. $350. Kill the carousel, add online ordering, make it mobile, add the Google-rich-result code so you show up better in search. Keeps your current site, just makes it work right. Doesn't get you the version you just saw though."

Always offer the **$1,500 Thursday slot** first. Most people pick the middle.

## The three time slots

These are the only three packages. Anything else is a custom job and you should price separately.

### Slot 1 — Quick Patch · 4 hours · $350

**For:** Owners who say "this is nice but I don't have the budget right now."

**You do:**
- Kill the auto-rotating carousel hero (replace with one strong photo)
- Replace the dated red script font with a modern serif
- Add a Toast/ChowNow ordering integration (or a clear "Order Online" link if they already have one)
- Fix the mobile nav and contact strip
- Add Restaurant schema (the hidden Google-rich-result code)
- Add a proper OG image so link-shares look like a real business

**You don't:**
- Rebuild the layout (their existing template stays)
- Touch the menu page (separate scope)
- Build out new sections (story, events, etc.)

**Time on-site:** 0 hours. You do this remote.
**Delivery:** Same week.

---

### Slot 2 — Full Rebuild · 8 hours · $1,500

**For:** Owners who saw the mockup and want THAT version.

**You do:**
- Drop the mockup you just showed them onto their domain
- Swap in their real logo (you already grabbed it)
- Confirm menu prices on-site, fix any that have changed
- Take 10-15 photos with your phone: interior, storefront, 4 food shots, owner/staff
- Replace the placeholder photos with the real ones
- Wire up online ordering (Toast / Slice / ChowNow — owner's choice)
- Add the favicon, OG image, schema, dynamic hours
- Push live to their domain
- Test on owner's phone before you leave

**Time on-site:** 2 hours. The rest is at your desk.
**Delivery:** Same day. Live by close of business.

---

### Slot 3 — Next-Day Rush · 24 hours · $2,400

**For:** Owners who say "can you do this faster?"

Same scope as Slot 2. Same deliverables. But you commit to live-by-tomorrow-night.

**You do:**
- Day 1: 4 hours on-site getting photos + menu confirm
- Day 1 evening: 4 hours building + deploying
- Day 2 morning: 2 hours owner walkthrough + minor edits + go live

**Why the price bump:** You're moving other work around. The premium is for the rush, not the build.

## Common objections + answers

**"How much is it ongoing?"**
> "Nothing. You own it forever. If you want me to update the menu monthly or do edits, that's $75 a month. Or you call me one-off whenever, $50 per edit. Most people don't need that."

**"What about hosting?"**
> "Free for the first year on Vercel. After that it's about $20 a month. I'll set up your account so it's in your name, not mine."

**"What about the domain?"**
> "You already own nypizzeriasalisbury.com. We point it at the new site. No new domain needed."

**"Can you do other things?"**
> "Photos, Google Business Profile updates, online ordering integration, branded merch — yeah. Let's get the site live first, then we can talk about whatever else."

**"My nephew said he'd do it for free."**
> "Cool. If he gets it done by next month, you don't need me. If he doesn't, call me. I'm not in a rush."

**"Can I think about it?"**
> "Yeah of course. Want me to send you the link so you can show your wife / business partner / whoever?"
> Then send the link and follow up in 3 days.

## What happens after they say yes

The first hour after a yes is the riskiest. Don't let them cool off.

1. **Right then:** Pull out your calendar. Confirm the slot. Write it on a card or text it to them.
2. **Same day:** Send a 1-page invoice (Stripe link). 50% deposit due before the install day. Other 50% on go-live.
3. **Day before the install:** Text the owner: "Tomorrow 11am, see you then." Keeps the slot warm.
4. **Day of:** Show up 15 minutes early. Coffee in hand. Phone charged.

## What's actually swappable in the template

For when you're on-site at a NEW restaurant (not DePietro's), here's what changes per client. Everything else stays:

| Section | What changes per client |
|---|---|
| `<title>` + meta description | Their name + city |
| Logo SVG | Their logo file |
| OG image URL | Their hero photo |
| Schema JSON-LD | Their name, address, phone, hours, cuisine |
| Hero kicker | City, MD · est. YYYY |
| Hero h1 | One sentence the owner agrees describes them |
| Hero sub | One paragraph the owner agrees with |
| Hero photo + alt | A strong photo of their food |
| Story h2 + body | Their actual story |
| Story stats (3 facts) | Three numbers that matter for them |
| Menu sections (HTML) | Their actual menu |
| Visit address + hours | Their actual address + hours |
| Footer mark + tag | Their name + one-liner |
| Tel: links | Their phone number |
| Maps link | Their address |

If you can scrape the existing site + spend 1 hour on-site, you have all of this. 8 hours of build time. $1,500 in. Repeatable.
